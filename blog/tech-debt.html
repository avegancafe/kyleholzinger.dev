Tech Debt And Its Uses

Feb 6, 2024

An explanation of my view of tech debt, when to use it, and when to address it

Tech debt is one of the most popular buzz words in the industry (behind "sizzle"). And surprisingly, it even extends beyond just engineering-- it frequently comes up in conversation with product managers, executives, sales, etc. So if it's so important that I have to talk about it when discussing projects with a CEO, then why does its definition feel so unclear?

TL;DR

Technical debt is a class of code defect that indicates that a feature was avoided due to its implementation being time-intensive. While this seems like it can be the reason for not implementing a lot of things, there is a distinct difference between de-scoping a feature because it's not really needed at the time, and a feature being avoided specifically because it would take a long time despite the desire to do it now. Technical debt is an inherently contentious decision. This "long time" measure is more of a feeling than a specific amount of time, but it's one of those things that you know it when you see it.
